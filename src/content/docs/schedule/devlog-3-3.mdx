        <h4>Summary</h4>
        <p>
            The chapter explains how data tracking is built into the basic functioning of the web and how user data is constantly collected, analyzed, and used to influence behavior. It shows that this process is part of a system of surveillance capitalism where corporations profit off of predicting people's actions. The chapter argues that data collection creates power imbalances, especially when users are unaware of how much information is being gathered and used against them. The example of gambling apps demonstrates how data tracking can actively exploit vulnerable people rather than help them.
        </p>

        <h4>Quote</h4>
        <p>
            "Surveillance capitalism unilaterally claims human experience as free raw material for translation into behavioral data." - Shoshana Zuboff
        </p>

        <h4>Comment</h4>
        <p>
            The quote at the beginning stood out to me because it clearly shows that the issue is not just data being collected, but it's that human behavior is becoming a resource to companies. The chapter clearly puts into words how online interactions are now resources that are sold off like any other physical material.
        </p>

        <h4>Question</h4>

        </p>
